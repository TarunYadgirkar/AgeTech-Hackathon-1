# Demo Scenarios

8 judge-ready input strings, 2–3 per tier. Used as one-click preset buttons in the UI.
The judge can also type their own — these just seed the demo.

---

## Minor — routine deviation, low urgency

**M1 — Unopened curtains**
> "Margaret hasn't opened her curtains and it's already 11am. She always opens them by 7."

*Why minor:* A delayed routine is a soft signal. No distress, no fall, no non-response — worth a gentle check-in.

---

**M2 — Skipped morning coffee**
> "Dad skipped his morning coffee again — second day in a row. He's been seeming a bit off."

*Why minor:* Behavioral change without urgency. Pattern worth noting; a voice check-in is proportionate.

---

## Medium — concerning, no immediate emergency

**Me1 — Stayed in bed past noon**
> "Margaret hasn't gotten out of bed and it's 1pm. She went to sleep at her normal time last night."

*Why medium:* Extended inactivity past a clear behavioral baseline. Not normal, not yet critical — multiple contacts should be reached.

---

**Me2 — Missed medication**
> "My father didn't take his blood pressure medication this morning. He never skips it."

*Why medium:* Medication non-adherence can cascade into a health event. Needs human follow-up soon but isn't an active emergency.

---

**Me3 — No movement in hours after recent fall**
> "Eleanor fell last week and the motion sensor shows she hasn't moved from the living room chair in over 4 hours."

*Why medium:* Context of recent fall elevates a long stillness period. Not an immediate 911 situation, but contact escalation is warranted.

---

## Major — potential active emergency

**Ma1 — On the floor, not responding**
> "Margaret is on the hallway floor and isn't responding when I call her name through the door."

*Why major:* Unresponsiveness plus a potentially injurious position. Every second matters — this goes to 911 intent if contacts don't answer immediately.

---

**Ma2 — Panic button, no answer on callback**
> "Dad pressed his emergency button and when I called back there was no answer. He's 84 and lives alone."

*Why major:* Self-initiated distress signal with a failed callback. The combination of an explicit alert and no follow-up contact is the clearest possible emergency signal.

---

**Ma3 — Smoke alarm, no response**
> "The smoke detector in my grandmother's apartment went off and she isn't picking up her phone. A neighbor says they can smell something."

*Why major:* Imminent physical danger. The environmental hazard plus non-response makes this a direct 911 scenario.

---

## Pitch script (spoken, ~2 min)

**Opening line:**
"Every elder-care detection system can notice that something's wrong. Almost none of them do anything about it."

**Walkthrough:**
1. "A judge types a scenario — something real, like 'Margaret is on the hallway floor and not responding.' Our classifier reads it, assigns a severity tier, and shows you exactly why."
2. "That tier maps to a procedure the caregiver already set up: call Margaret first, then her daughter Sarah, then — if no one answers — 911 intent. The system walks it step by step with live timers."
3. "The 911 state is shown on screen. We never dial a real number. This is a demo guardrail, not a product limitation."
4. "Now watch what happens with a minor scenario: skipped curtains at 11am. Different tier, completely different procedure. The severity reasoning is right there on screen."

**Closing:**
"Detection without follow-through is just an alert that nobody acted on. We close that gap."
